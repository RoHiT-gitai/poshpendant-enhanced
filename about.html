Why Jaipur?

A City Built on
Gemstones & Gold

Jaipur wasn't just built — it was designed. Founded in 1727 by Maharaja Jai Singh II, the Pink City was conceived as a centre of trade, art and craftsmanship. The Maharaja specifically invited gem traders and jewellers from across the Mughal empire to settle here, gifting them entire neighbourhoods — a tradition that has never stopped.

Today, the Johari Bazaar district in the heart of the city is the world's single largest open-air gemstone market. Thousands of family-run workshops line narrow lanes where raw stones arrive from mines in Africa, Brazil, and across India — and leave as finished jewels bound for Paris, New York and Tokyo.

What makes Jaipur irreplaceable isn't just scale — it's generational knowledge. Gem-cutting techniques like kundan (pure gold setting) and meenakari (enamel work) have been passed father to son for centuries. These aren't skills you learn in a factory. They live in the hands of families who have done nothing else for 300 years.

🏛️
Royal Patronage Since 1727
Maharaja Jai Singh II made Jaipur India's first planned city and established gemstone trade as its economic backbone. Royal workshops produced jewels for Mughal courts, eventually supplying European royalty too.
💎
The Johari Bazaar Effect
Being in Jaipur means PoshPendant sources directly from the same markets where global luxury brands buy — at a fraction of the cost, with zero middlemen. Every rupee saved goes into stone quality, not supply chain markups.
🤲
Kundan & Meenakari Mastery
Our artisans are trained in kundan setting — the art of embedding gemstones in pure gold foil — a 600-year-old technique that produces the most secure, luminous stone settings in the world.
🌍
Global Export Hub
Jaipur exports over ₹20,000 crore of gems and jewellery annually. Brands like Cartier, Bulgari and Chopard quietly source stones and craftwork from this city. We bring that same quality — directly to you.
Sourced at the Source

Every Stone We Use
Comes from Here

We don't import finished gemstones. We walk Jaipur's markets ourselves, selecting raw stones by hand before they ever reach a cutting wheel. This means we control quality at every stage — from the moment a stone leaves the earth to the moment it arrives at your door.

Jaipur cuts 70% of the world's coloured gemstones — jade from Myanmar, moonstone from Sri Lanka, rose quartz from Brazil, amethyst from Zambia. They all pass through this city. Being here means we get first pick, at source price, with full traceability.

Behind the Craft

How Every Piece
Comes to Life

From raw gemstone to a piece you'll wear every day — here's the journey inside our Jaipur workshop.

✏️
1. Designed in Jaipur
Each design starts as a hand sketch in our Jaipur studio. We spend weeks refining proportions and the feeling a piece should carry before a single stone is touched.
💎
2. Gemstones Selected
Natural stones are hand-selected from Jaipur's gemstone markets — jade, moonstone, rose quartz, bloodstone. Only stones that meet our clarity and colour standards make it in.
⚒️
3. Set by Hand
Skilled artisans set each gemstone individually in 18K gold-plated brass. No machines, no shortcuts. Each piece takes 2–6 hours to complete.
✨
4. Export-Quality Finish
Before leaving our workshop, every piece passes a 12-point quality check — finish, clasp integrity, plating consistency, and the final polish that makes it shine.
